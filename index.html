How the risk is calculated

Each pit sector is scored with an infinite-slope limit-equilibrium model. Factor of Safety FoS = τresisting / τdriving, where rainfall and dewatering set the pore-water pressure u, and blasting adds a pseudo-static load. FoS < 1.0 indicates failure conditions. Sectors differ in depth, drainage and rock strength, so risk is spatial — as in the field.

Consequence · why the early warning matters

Failure scenario — what is at stake below

When the factor of safety falls below 1.0, the wedge above the failure plane lets go. This is the runout into the working pit, where haul trucks and crews operate. Severity scales with the live factor of safety from the twin.
